Dejene Zewdie

Researching the formation and evolution of galaxies and AGNs.

Since March 2024, I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Centre for Space Research at North-West University, South Africa. My research focuses on studying stellar populations Fornax cluster galaxies and environmental effects in connection with gas content. And also, I survey Lyman break Galaxies in the vicinity of extreme luminous quasars to better characterize the richness and evolutionary state of their environment. I obtained my Ph.D. from the Instituto de Estudios Astrofísicos at Universidad Diego Portales in Santiago, Chile, working with Prof. Roberto Assef.

Research Interests

- High-redshift quasar environment
- Morphological evolution of galaxies
- Star formation and AGN activity
- Large Scale Structure formation and evolution

My research seeks to understand the large-scale environments and the formation and evolution of massive galaxies. I use deep imaging and spectroscopic observations to explore the environments of extremely luminous, obscured quasars and to characterize their evolutionary states. I am also investigating the connection between star formation and active galactic nuclei (AGNs) activity at lower redshifts, aiming to shed light on the interplay between these processes in shaping galaxy evolution.

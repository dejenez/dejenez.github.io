Research Projects

My research focuses on galaxy evolution, large-scale structure, and the environments of luminous quasars. Below are selected research projects.

Hot DOGs as Tracers of Dense Cosmic Environments

We investigate the environments of hyperluminous dust-obscured quasars (Hot DOGs) using deep imaging. We identify overdensities of Lyman-break galaxies, revealing that these systems trace dense regions in the early Universe.

Fornax Cluster Stellar Populations

This project studies stellar populations and environmental effects in Fornax cluster galaxies, focusing on gas content and galaxy evolution.

MUSE Spectroscopic Studies

Using deep MUSE observations, we confirm Lyman-alpha emitters and study faint galaxy populations in high-redshift environments.
